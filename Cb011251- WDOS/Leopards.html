Leopards in Sri Lanka

Leopards are native to Sri Lanka, and they play a significant role in the country's wildlife. Sri Lankan Leopard (Panthera pardus kotiya),
The leopard species found in Sri Lanka is a subspecies known as the Sri Lankan leopard. The Sri Lankan leopard is endemic to the island,
meaning it is found nowhere else in the world except Srilanka. Sri Lankan leopards have a golden-yellow coat with dark spots and rosettes.
Their coat is adapted to the diverse habitats they inhabit, ranging from dry zones to rainforests. Yala National Park is known for having one
of the highest leopard population densities globally. It provides a prime location for leopard sightings. Leopards are primarily nocturnal, being
more active during the night. However, they may also be spotted during the early morning and late evening. Leopards are skilled climbers
and often use trees to rest, survey their surroundings, or stash prey out of reach of other predators. Sri Lankan leopards are highly adaptable
and can thrive in a variety of environments, from dense forests to open savannas. The Sri Lankan leopard faces threats such as habitat loss,
human-wildlife conflict, and poaching. Conservation efforts are in place to protect these leopards and their habitats. Leopard safaris in national
parks like Yala and Wilpattu are popular among tourists, providing opportunities to observe these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat.
