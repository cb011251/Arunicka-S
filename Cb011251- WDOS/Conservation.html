Department Of Wildlife Conservation

Providing physical protection to wildlife is among the most high-risk professions in the conservation sector as it is directly associated with
the prevention of wildlife crimes. In Sri Lanka, the Department of Wildlife Conservation is the primary government agency responsible for the
long-term conservation and protection of biological diversity. Since the establishment of the department in October 1949, there have been
casualties in the line of duty among field officers of DWC. Here, we examine the nature and the conditions that led to the demise of these
field officers whilst on duty. From October 1949 to December 2020, at least 80 have been killed and one reported missing while on duty.
The death rate averaged one officer per year. Sri Lanka has a government department known as the Department of Wildlife Conservation.
The primary responsibility of this department is the conservation and management of wildlife and their habitats across the country. Sri Lanka
is known for its rich biodiversity, including a variety of flora and fauna.
The country is home to numerous species of mammals, birds, reptiles, and amphibians. The Department of Wildlife Conservation engages in
research and monitoring activities to assess the status of different species, understand ecosystems, and implement effective conservation
strategies. Sri Lanka has established several national parks, wildlife sanctuaries, and reserves to protect and conserve its diverse ecosystems.
Examples include Yala National Park, Wilpattu National Park, and Sinharaja Forest Reserve. Like many countries, Sri Lanka faces challenges
related to the conservation of endangered species. Efforts are made to protect and preserve species such as the Sri Lankan elephant, leopard,
and various endemic birds.
